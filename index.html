Exercises à la Barre

Pliés

"Pliés perfect the equilibrium and render the joints and muscles soft and pliable." (p. 57)

Level Time Signature Starts Intro Pattern Positions Transition Ending Common Faults
Grade 1
6/8 (Moderato)

The 6/8 provides a lengthened phrase, perhaps to reduce the chance of the dancer's movement becoming static?

 Facing the barre, in 1st Tendu to 2nd
(5 hands, 6 tendu, 7 place)		 3 demi-pliés 2nd, 1st, repeat 7 8 (tendu to 2nd) Rise, leave hands on barre, lower after music
  • loss of turnout (#1)
  • inappropriate distance from the barre
  • inappropriate connection with the barre: connection should be fingertips only!
Grade 2
3/4 (Largo)

?

 Facing the barre, in 1st Tendu to 2nd
Rise, demi, demi The rise before the pliés brings the placement of weight to the front of the foot and acts as a test of balance. The student lengthens up before the plié and begins to train the landing for allegro with control.
2nd, 1st, 5th devant, 5th derrière

Reiterates the traditional sequence of positions for pliés

 7 8 (tendu to 2nd) Close back to 1st
  • Anticipating the plié before heels are fully lowered
Grade 3
6/8 (Larghetto)

Has a longer musical phrase: this might relate to dancer maturity.

 One hand to barre, in 5th Tendu to 2nd Demi, rise, demi 2nd, 1st, 5th devant, 5th derrière 7 8 (tendu to 2nd) Close back to 5th devant
  • Popping heels
Grade 4
6/8 (Andante) or 4/4?

?

 One hand to barre, in 5th Tendu to 2nd Demi, grand (2nd, 1st)
Demi, rise, demi (5th)		 2nd, 1st, 5th devant, 5th derrière 7 8 (tendu to 2nd) Close back to 5th devant
  • Tipping pelvis in the grand plié
  • Going too low in grand plié
  • Lifting heels too quickly & not putting them down soon enough in grand plié in 1st
  • Not understanding the difference between demi and grand in 2nd
Grade 5
4/4 (Andantino)

The 4/4 makes it very much like traditional pliés

 One hand to barre, in 5th Tendu to 2nd Grand (new in 5th), rise with first port de bras, lower arm with tendu closing 2nd, 1st, 5th devant, 5th derrière & 8 (tendu to 2nd) Close back to 5th devant

Battements Tendus

"Battements Tendus serve to stretch and strengthen the muscles, to force the insteps well outwards, and to enable you to slide out and return your foot in the same straight line." (p. 58)

Level Time Signature Starts Intro Pattern Positions Ending Common Faults
Grade 1
4/4 (March)

?

 Facing the barre, in 1st Hands onto barre Tendu, stretch, stretch, close (twice each side, repeat) Tendus to 2nd, closing to 1st Hands off the barre
  • not working through feet
  • transferring weight onto gesture foot
  • bending supporting knee
  • clenched, sickled, or rolling feet
  • pelvic alignment on supporting leg
Grade 2
4/4 (March)

?

 One hand to the barre, in 5th Hand onto barre Tendu, close (even timing) En croix, closing to 5th Hand off the barre
  • not tenduing to a crossed position
  • not maintaining turnout equally
  • not working through feet
  • transferring weight onto gesture foot
  • bending supporting knee (because weight is too far back or the calf is getting in the way)
  • clenched, sickled, or rolling feet
  • pelvic alignment on supporting leg
Grade 3 (Demi-Plié with Battements Tendus)
6/8 (Barcarolle)

A Barcarolle moves and should be soothing

 Facing the barre, in 1st Hands onto barre Demi-plié for 2, stretch tendu for 2, 2 counts close into plie in 1st, stretch for 2 (other side, and repeat all)
And a bend and bend and tendu to stretch and a bend and a bend and stretch up! 		Tendus to 2nd, closing in 1st Hands off the barre
Grade 3 (Battements Tendus from 5th Position)
2/4 (Andante)

?

 One hand to barre, in 5th Hand onto barre
Front, side, back, plié (reverse)

Create contrast of dynamics between the tendu and plié. Also increase in the volune of tendus, so there is more standing time on the supporting leg.

 Tendus to 4th and 2nd, closing to 5th Hand off the barre
Grade 4
2/4 (Hornpipe)

?

 One hand to barre, in 5th Hand onto barre Tendu 2nd, stretch, stretch, close (twice), tendu close (twice), plié, stretch (repeat) Tendus to 2nd, closing in 5th Hand off the barre
Grade 5
2/2 (March)

?

 One hand to barre, in 5th Hand onto barre 4 tendus with Cecchetti accent, temps lié (bend, bend, bend, stretch!), temps lié (repeat all)
Don't put your hand back on the barre on the last temps lié 		Tendus to 2nd, closing in 5th Close after the music

Battements Dégagés

"Battements Dégagés serve to render the insteps supple, to strengthen the toes,and to strengthen and render elastic the muscles so that you may attain a good élévation, that is, the power to jump high in the air. (p. 58)

Level Time Signature Starts Intro Pattern Positions Ending Common Faults
Grade 3
2/4 (Allegretto)

One hand to the barre, in 1st Tendu to 2nd 7 battements dégagés, even timing Dégagés to 2nd, closing in 1st Hand off the barre
Grade 4
2/4 (Allegretto)

One hand to the barre, in 1st Tendu to 2nd 6 battements dégagés, plié, stretch into dégagé (repeat 3 times) Dégagés to 2nd, closing in 1st Close after the music
Grade 5
2/4 (Allegretto)

One hand to barre, in 5th Tendu to 2nd 2 1-count dégagés to 1st, 1 2-count dégagés to 5th (derrière, then repeat devant), 8 1-count dégagés to alternating 5ths Dégagés to 2nd, closing in 1st and 5th Hand off the barre

Ronds de Jambe à Terre

"Ronds de Jambe à Terre serve to enable you to turn your leg well outwards, to keep the point of the toe well back and the heel well forwards. (p. 58)

Level Time Signature Starts Intro Pattern Ending Common Faults
Grade 2
3/8 (Moderato)

One hand to the barre, in 1st Arms to 2nd Tendu devant, demi-rond, hold, close. Tendu to 2nd, demi-rond en dehors, hold, close. Reverse. Return arms to 5th en bas
Grade 3
3/4 (Moderato)

One hand to the barre, in 1st Arms to 2nd 2 ronds de jambe à en dehors with a slight pause in each position. (Repeat en dedans) Full rond de jambes are new Return arms to 5th en bas
Grade 4
3/4 (Moderato)

One hand to barre, in 1st Arms to 2nd 4-count rond de jambe en dehors, closing to 1st, lowering arm to 5th.
Demi-plié, 4-count rond de jambe en dehors on a fondu, open arm to 2nd. (Repeat en dedans)
4-count forward bend, 2-count rise moving arm to open 5th en haut, balance in open 5th en haut (2 counts) 		Lower after the music, arms through 2nd
Grade 5
3/4 (Moderato)

One hand to barre, in 5th Cecchetti preparation for rond de jambe 4 rond de jambe en dehors, 3 rond de jambe en dedans Close into 5th with music, arms en bas

Battements Frappés

"Battements Frappés sur le cou-de-pied serve to strengthen the insteps, to strengthen the toes, and to strengthen and render the muscles elastic, so that you may attain a good élévation. (p. 58)

Level Time Signature Starts Intro Pattern Ending Common Faults
Grade 3
2/4 (Galop)

One hand to barre, in 5th Tendu to 2nd, place foot sur le cou-de-pied devant 8 single frappés (out hold hold in (1 & 2 &), except the last, finishing out) Close into 5th after the music
Grade 4
2/4 (Galop)

One hand to barre, in 5th Tendu to 2nd, place foot sur le cou-de-pied devant 8 singles then 4 doubles, each with a hold Close after the music
Grade 5
2/4 (Galop)

One hand to barre, in 5th Tendu to 2nd, place foot sur le cou-de-pied devant 3 singles, hold, repeat. 3 doubles, hold, repeat. (First time doing doubles without holding after) Close after the music
